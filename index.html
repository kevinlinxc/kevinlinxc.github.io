PROGRAMMING

OmenBot

Python, Discord.py, Open Notify API, Youtube_DL, FFmpeg | August 2020

OmenBot is a Discord.py bot that I made for use in my friends' voice calls. If you're unaware, Discord is a voice/text chat application. My bot is primarily a music bot, capable of playing audio from Youtube urls or Youtube search queries, pausing, stopping and resuming. Additionally, it plays some funny sound effects into voice calls and tells our friend George to do his homework. This isn't the same as the marketed and published Omen Bot, it's just the name I gave my personal bot. 

PlayBarnesBot

Python, Tweepy, Pytrends, AWS Lambda | January 2020

In the video game Hearthstone, there is a card named Barnes that summons other creatures. A content creator that I enjoy watching popularized the catchphrase "I play Barnes and I summon ___". I decided to make a Twitter bot that finishes the phrase in daily tweets using words from Google Trends, Cards Against Humanity cards, and some custom words. To automate the script to run daily, I learned to use Amazon Web Services (AWS).

Autoscroll Sidekick

Java, Android Studio | November 2019

While learning to play the guitar, I was frustrated that the automatic scrolling of guitar tabs (sheet music) was locked behind a paywall on some popular websites and applications. Using the experience from my internship, I made my own app that could take pictures and scroll through them at a modifiable speed. After a year of leaving this project on the backburner, I completed it and published my app onto the Google Play Store.

MECHANICAL/ELECTRICAL

ENPH Robot Summer 2020

Onshape, Circuit Design, Soldering, STM-32 BluePill, C++ PID Software | May - August 2020

Every year, Engineering Physics has a robot summer course where a robot is built to achieve a goal in a competition with classmates in teams of 4. Our cohort's competition was at home because of COVID-19, so we picked up electronics from the university and bought tools/materials to make our robot. I learned to design and solder circuits, write tape-following PID software in C++, use various sensors/motors, and construct a chassis using hot glue and plastic. Tablet Pen

Solidworks | December 2019

The tablet that I use to take notes came with a very small stylus, about the size of a Nintendo DS stylus. I decided I wanted to modify to be more ergonomic and easier to hold. I experimented with a few pen bodies, and then designed and 3D printed an insert that lets the stylus stick with the pen body and provide a comfortable grip. I use it in class to take notes now.


Robotic Claw

Arduino Solidworks | January 2019

For my APSC 101 class, my team and I got to build a robotic claw using aluminum sheets, pop rivets, hobby sonar sensors, and Arduino software. The claw was designed and built over a month of work, and in the end it was capable of picking up 1-5Kg blocks, large scoops of pasta, and a really long stick. The claw uses the sonar sensor to detect when to open, and then releases again when it detects a bin to drop the contents into.
