PROGRAMMING

OmenBot

Python, Discord.py, Open Notify API, Youtube_DL, FFmpeg | August 2020

OmenBot is a Discord.py bot that I made for use in my friends' voice calls. If you're unaware, Discord is a voice/text chat application. My bot is primarily a music bot, capable of playing audio from Youtube urls or Youtube search queries. Additionally, it plays some funny sound effects into voice calls and tells our friend George to do his homework. This isn't the same as the marketed and published Omen Bot, it's just the name I gave my personal bot. 

PlayBarnesBot

Python, Tweepy, Pytrends, AWS Lambda | January 2020

In the video game Hearthstone, there is a card named Barnes that summons other creatures. A content creator that I enjoy watching popularized the catchphrase "I play Barnes and I summon ___". I decided to make a Twitter bot that finishes the phrase in daily tweets using words from Google Trends, Cards Against Humanity cards, and some custom words. To automate the script to run daily, I learned to use Amazon Web Services (AWS).

Guitar Autoscroll

Java, Android Studio | November 2019

While learning to play the guitar, I was frustrated that the automatic scrolling of guitar tabs (sheet music) was locked behind a paywall on some popular websites and applications. Using the experience from my internship, I made my own app that could take pictures and scroll through them at a modifiable speed. I didn't learn to optimize applications, so I never made more than a prototype on my phone, and I decided not to release the app.

NurseAnnieBot

Python, Numpy, OpenCV | July 2019

Another content creator that I follow made a simple video game in which you have to recognize specific inputs and respond with your own keyboard inputs. I used some basic computer vision to automate playing of the game as an exercise and a minor introduction to computer vision. I plan on attempting another computer vision project soon, since this project only had basic color matching.

MECHANICAL/ELECTRICAL

ENPH Robot Summer 2020

Onshape, Circuit Design, Soldering, STM-32 BluePill, C++ PID Software | May - August 2020

Every year, Engineering Physics has a robot summer course where a robot is built to achieve a goal in a competition with classmates in teams of 4. Our cohort's competition was at home because of COVID-19, so we picked up electronics from the university and bought tools/materials to make our robot. I learned to design and solder circuits, write tape-following PID software in C++, use various sensors/motors, and construct a chassis using hot glue and plastic.
